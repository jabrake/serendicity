SERENDICITY

What is serendipity? Something that is lacking when we travel, now more than ever. The Internet has a wealth of information, but this very abundance leads to rigid checklists, schedules, and itineraries when traveling. Traveling lends itself to serendipitous encounters, with places and strangers. The Internet may have minimized the chance for serendipity, but it can also increase it.

What is it?

Serendicity seeks to bring serendipity back into travel. It's a mobile app that encourages serendipitous exploration and discovery of city neighborhoods. By providing the most interesting walking route to your destination, rather than the fastest one, the app will nudge you to stray from your intended course, if only for a few minutes.

How does it work?

Serendicity leverages APIs from Foursquare and Google Maps. The app harnesses the Internet's wealth of information to map personalized walking routes in three simple steps: find your destination, pick a category of stops, and indicate how much time you have.
