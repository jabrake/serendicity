SERENDICITY

ser·en·di·ci·ty ( /serən'disitē/ noun ) :
A mobile app that encourages serendipitous exploration and discovery of city neighborhoods

What is Serendicity?

Serendicity is an attempt to upend the normative travel experience. Travelers often have a specific destination in mind when they arrive to a new city. Rather than taking the fastest route there, why not explore a more interesting one?

Leveraging open APIs and frameworks, the mobile app generates walking routes based on location, time, and preferences. The end result: less structure, more serendipity, and hopefully a different travel experience.

Why should you use Serendicity?

The Internet provides us with a wealth of information and ways to self-curate everything we do, from the media we consume and the food we eat, to our circle of friends. The same phenomenon applies to travel: we arm ourselves with information and research about our destinations before we even get there. This often leads to trips structured with checklists, schedules, itineraries, and high expectations.

Serendicity aims to bring serendipity back into travel. Leaving things up to chance can be a good thing.

How does it work?

Three simple steps: find your destination, pick a category of stops, and indicate how much time you have. Serendicity uses Foursquare's API for location information, and the Google Maps API to create personalized walking routes.
